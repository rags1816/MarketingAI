# Marketing AI Suite — Methodology

## Origin

Developed by Vijay L Narasimhan, 2025–26, as a direct structural mirror of
the Procure AI Category Suite's governed logic — **Market Levels → Drivers →
Strategy → Plan → Execution** — applied to B2B marketing and sales strategy
rather than procurement category strategy. Built to take a founder or
marketer from a raw idea to a board-ready strategy using the same
level-based, driver-led discipline as the procurement suite, rather than a
free-form AI chat.

## Problem this solves

Most AI marketing-strategy tools generate plausible-sounding output from a
single prompt, with no structure forcing the user to establish market
context before jumping to tactics, and no traceability from a
recommendation back to the evidence that justified it. This suite forces a
governed sequence — market understanding, then drivers, then strategic
choice, then execution — so every downstream recommendation can be traced
back to a specific driver or piece of market evidence, mirroring the
discipline expected in category management.

## Built on / credited frameworks

This suite embeds a substantial number of established consulting and
marketing frameworks as the analytical engine within each module, rather
than replacing them with a proprietary model:

- **PESTEL** — macro-environment scan (Level 1 / Module 1)
- **Porter's Five Forces** — industry/market structure (Level 2 / Module 1)
- **Porter's Generic Strategies** — core strategic posture choice (Module 4)
- **Ansoff Matrix** — growth direction (Module 4)
- **STP (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning)** — Module 4/6
- **SWOT → TOWS** — competitive/internal position (Module 3)
- **VRIO** — capability/right-to-win test (Level 5)
- **Kapferer's Brand Identity Prism** and **Keller's CBBE Pyramid** — brand
  strategy (Module 5)
- **7Ps** — marketing mix (Module 7)
- **RACE** — digital channel planning framework (Module 8)
- **SOSTAC** — the plan assembly wrapper (Module 10)
- **ICP scorecards, ABM tiering, DMU personas** — B2B account and buying-
  committee segmentation (Module 6)

What this suite contributes is not the frameworks themselves (all
well-established, publicly attributable methods) but the **governed
sequencing, cascading context, and decision-gate discipline** that forces
them to be applied in the right order, with each module's output feeding
the next — the same architectural pattern as the Category AI suite, applied
to a different domain.

## Core framework (your original model)

**Market Levels model** (the structural backbone, mirroring category
management's level-based analysis):
- Level 1 — Macro Environment (PESTEL)
- Level 2 — Industry/Market (Five Forces, TAM/SAM/SOM)
- Level 3 — Competitive Set (strategic groups, benchmarking)
- Level 4 — Customer (segmentation, Jobs-to-be-Done, DMU)
- Level 5 — Company & Brand (SWOT, VRIO, brand equity audit)

**Driver-led logic:** every driver (demand, supply/competitive, cost/price,
channel/media, macro) is scored on Impact × Certainty × Time horizon and
plotted on an Impact–Certainty matrix; high-impact/high-certainty drivers
are what the strategy is built on. Every downstream recommendation must
trace back to a named driver — this traceability rule is the suite's core
governance mechanism.

**Four decision gates** enforce that the user (not just the AI) makes and
records the strategic calls: market attractiveness, strategic posture (Porter
+ Ansoff), ICP/positioning lock, and budget envelope — each requiring a
written decision note captured in the final document.

## Inputs → Process → Outputs

- **Inputs:** 15-question guided intake (idea, vertical, ACV, sales-cycle
  length, DMU complexity, budget band, constraints)
- **Process:** cascading AI-drafted modules, each conditioned on the intake
  brief plus all prior module outputs, gated by four confirmation points
- **Outputs:** a SOSTAC-assembled Strategy Document (Situation, Objectives,
  Strategy, Tactics, Action, Control) plus a phased 12-month roadmap,
  downloadable as Markdown

## Why this approach (rationale)

A single AI prompt can produce a marketing strategy that sounds complete but
skips market validation, ignores competitive reality, or recommends tactics
unsupported by any driver. Forcing level-based sequencing and driver
traceability — the same discipline applied in procurement category
strategy — produces a strategy that can be defended and interrogated, not
just read.
